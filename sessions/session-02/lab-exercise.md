# Lab Exercise: Create Farm Issues

## Session 2 — GitHub Issues for Task Tracking

> **Time:** 35 minutes
> **Mode:** Independent practice
> **What you need:** Browser open to the demo farm repository, logged into your GitHub account

---

## Choose Your Farm Scenario

- **Option A: Crop Farming** — Focus on planting and field tasks
- **Option B: Livestock** — Focus on animal care tasks
- **Option C: Equipment & Maintenance** — Focus on repair and upkeep tasks

---

## Part 1: Create Your First Issue (12 minutes)

**Instructions:**

1. Go to the demo farm repository in your browser
2. Click the **"Issues"** tab (near the top of the page)
3. Click the green **"New issue"** button
4. Fill in the following:

**For Crop Farming (Option A):**
- **Title:** "Prepare Field 3 for spring corn planting"
- **Description:** Write 2-3 sentences about what needs to be done. Include:
  - Which field
  - What crop
  - Target planting date

**For Livestock (Option B):**
- **Title:** "Schedule Herd B annual vaccinations"
- **Description:** Write 2-3 sentences about what needs to be done. Include:
  - Which animals
  - What vaccinations
  - Deadline date

**For Equipment (Option C):**
- **Title:** "Tractor #2 — 500-hour service due"
- **Description:** Write 2-3 sentences about what needs to be done. Include:
  - Which equipment
  - What service is needed
  - When it's due

5. Upload a photo: Click the **paperclip icon** at the bottom of the description box and select a sample photo (provided by instructor) OR use any photo from your device. **Can't find a photo?** Check your Downloads folder, or skip this step for now — you can add a photo later by editing the Issue.
6. Click **"Submit new issue"**

**Checkpoint:** Your Issue should appear with a number (like #5 or #12).

- **Question 1:** What number was assigned to your Issue?
- Answer: _____

---

## Part 2: Add Labels and Assign (5 minutes)

**Instructions:**

7. On your Issue page, look at the right side of the page
8. Click **"Labels"** and select the label that fits:

| Scenario | Recommended Labels |
|----------|-------------------|
| Crop Farming | crops, routine |
| Livestock | livestock, health |
| Equipment | equipment, maintenance |

9. Click **"Assignees"** and assign the Issue to yourself

**Checkpoint:** Your Issue should show colored label badges and your name as assignee.

- **Question 2:** What is the name of the label you added? (e.g., equipment, crops)
- Answer: _____

---

## Part 3: Create Two More Issues (13 minutes)

**Instructions:**

10. Go back to the Issues tab (click "Issues" in the tab bar)
11. Create **Issue #2** using a different task from your scenario:

**Option A suggestions:** "Test soil pH in Field 1", "Order soybean seeds for north section"
**Option B suggestions:** "Deworm calves before turnout", "Check water trough heating element"
**Option C suggestions:** "Fix broken fence post — north pasture", "Replace irrigation nozzle in Field 2"

12. Create **Issue #3** — choose any farm task you want! This one should be from your real farm if possible. **Need an idea?** Try one of these:
    - "Clean chicken coop before new chicks arrive"
    - "Replace worn belts on combine before harvest"
    - "Repair gate latch on south field"
    - "Test well water quality this month"

13. Add a label to each new Issue.

**Checkpoint:** You should have 3 Issues in the repository with your name on them.

---

## Part 4: Comment and Close (5 minutes)

**Instructions:**

14. Open your **Issue #1** (click on its title in the Issue list)

15. Add a comment with an update. Examples:
    - "Ordered the seeds. Expected delivery: March 15."
    - "Called the vet — appointment scheduled for April 2."
    - "Changed the oil and filters. Logged 502 hours on meter."

16. Add a second comment to simulate completing the task:
    - "Task complete. Field 3 is prepped and ready for planting."
    - "All vaccinations administered. Records updated."
    - "Service complete. Next service due at 1000 hours."

17. Click **"Close with comment"** on Issue #1 to mark it as done.

18. Go back to the Issues tab. Click **"Closed"** to see your closed Issue.

**Checkpoint:** Issue #1 is closed. Issues #2 and #3 are still open.

- **Question 3:** How many open Issues do you have? _____
- **Question 4:** How many closed Issues do you have? _____

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Can't find the Issues tab | It's in the tab bar near the top of the repository page, between "Code" and "Pull requests" |
| "New issue" button is grayed out | Make sure you're logged in. Check that you have access to this repository. |
| Photo won't upload | Check file size (must be under 10MB). Try a different photo format (JPG, PNG work best). |
| Can't find labels | After creating the Issue, look at the right side of the page. Click "Labels" to see the list. |
| Can't find my photo to upload | Check your Downloads folder or Pictures folder. On a phone, try the camera icon to take a new photo. |
| Made a typo in the title | Click the three-dot menu (...) next to the Issue title, then click "Edit" to fix it. |
| Accidentally closed an Issue | Click "Reopen issue" at the bottom of the Issue page. |
| Can't see other people's Issues | Make sure you're on the Issues tab and "Open" is selected. |
